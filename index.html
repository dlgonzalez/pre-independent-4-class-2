📄 Click to Read: "Ask an Expert" by Prof. Anna Felix

Q: Is talent genetic, or is it just practice?

A: This is a debate that has lasted for centuries. Personally, I believe that hard work is far more important than genetics. Some researchers claim that "geniuses are born, not made," but I disagree.

Let's look at the data. A famous study by Dr. K. Anders Ericsson investigated violinists at a music academy. The research showed that the best violinists had practiced for over 10,000 hours by age 20. The average students had only practiced for 4,000 hours. This proves that practice is essential.

However, recent DNA studies on identical twins show that genetics do play a role in physical abilities like lung capacity and muscle type. Therefore, you might inherit the potential to be a great runner, but you won't win a gold medal without training.

In my opinion, we rely too much on the excuse of "I'm not talented." Anyone can achieve greatness if they put in the hours.

📘 Grammar: Reported Speech - Statements (Page 115)
When we repeat what someone else said, we usually shift the verb tense "one step back in time" (e.g., Present becomes Past). Before doing the book exercises, review the Tabloid Reporter's notes to see this in action, then complete our 15-item digital clinic.

Inductive Discovery: The Tabloid Reporter

Notice how the reporter changes the celebrity's exact words when writing the news article.

⭐ "I am very happy today."
She told me that she was very happy that day. 📰
⭐ "We are learning to surf."
They said that they were learning to surf. 📰
⭐ "I will start a new movie."
He mentioned that he would start a new movie. 📰
⭐ "I can play the guitar."
She said that she could play the guitar. 📰

Grammar Guide: The Backshift Rule

Direct Speech (The Quote) Reported Speech (The Article)
Present Simple (I play)➔Past Simple (He played)
Present Continuous (I am playing)➔Past Continuous (He was playing)
Will (I will play)➔Would (He would play)
Can (I can play)➔Could (He could play)
